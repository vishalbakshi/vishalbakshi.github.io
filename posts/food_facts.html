ObjectiveÂ¶

Open Food Facts is an open-sourced database of nutritional information for food products across the world. People can add a food product by uploading a picture of the bar code, nutrition facts label, or just the product. The CSV file is ~600MB so Google Sheets could not store it, OpenOffice could but was relatively very slow and pandas quite easily managed the data.

I am quite stressed with validating the data of a new data set, primarily since this is a logical first step, but also because I do not want to start to analyze, make claims and subsequently get excited before I confirm that what I'm working with is relatively legitimate. This post focuses on that first step. I've also taken (too much) time to wrap my head around subsetting DataFrames with the fillna so some extra practice I've done with that is also linked.

def PlotVertLine(xarr, cdf, ax):
    index = np.where(cdf>0.99)
    x99 = xarr[index[0][0]]
    ax.plot([x99, x99], [0, 1])
    ax.text(120, 0.5, '99th %-ile: ' + str(x99) + 'g')
