freewriting.

if you were to be inside my mind, which you are in a way by reading this (although writing is just one type of approximation or projection i use to map what's going on in my mind to what's going on outside of it), you would know that there is an ubiquitous (my current favorite word) drive towards clarity in logic, reasoning and its communication to others. one such form of this drive is to better understand complexity. nothing is as complex as communicating an emotion experienced in your hardware (i wonder if emotions are to the body what thoughts are to the mind? some kind of data? random information?) from your perspective and perceptions to someone else's mind (made of their perceptions) without any direct channel of streaming thoughts and emotions to them. another is my drive toward simplicity (both expressions and sources of it). nothing is simpler than nature. the deafening quiet of a forest, even the smallest one in your local park, comes from the wildly active ecosystem of roots and mycelium, soil, nutrients, insects, mammals, and plants actively buzzing with life---sending each other signals, transforming chemicals from one form to another, gathering, eating, fucking, or moving in the wind, absorbing raindrops, warming in the sun. but it's wrapped in silence. peace. tranquility. i think that's why it feels so awesome and intimidating. the silence of nature is heavy, powerful.

simplicity is one of my favorite communication characteristics. from the perspective of a teacher and a student, simplicity is the holy grail. i know i've done my job if i explained it simply, you know you've done yours if you either understood a simple explanation or did not and gave me feedback on it. this back and forth creates a powerful environment for knowledge to transfer between two individuals. (by "understand" i mean being able to look at it from different perspectives while maintaining consistency and completeness of your logic and knowledge). this can look like applying the knowledge to some other context (than the one from which you got it), or steel man opposing perspectives to toughen your theory and knowledge.

simplicity alone does not guarantee honest, accuracy and comprehensiveness. those are additional characteristics that must be exhibited in tandem with simplicity for the communication to be effective, in my opinion.

the complexity of human emotions and the simplicity of nature are two overarching concepts, two poles, across which there is a vast potential of creative opportunities.
