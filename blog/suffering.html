Viktor Frankl in his book "Man's Search for Meaning" (that will hopefully inspire many a blog post of mine) tells the reader that unavoidable suffering can have meaning, but avoidable suffering cannot.

avoidable suffering, if pursued, is masochism. unavoidable suffering if endured can surface a deeper, richer, fundamental, inimitable meaning of life.

i imagine two similary related concepts on the other end of the spectrum: attainable joy and unattainable joy. pursuing unattainable joy might be related to enduring avoidable suffering.

pursuing an unattainable joy and enduring an avoidable suffering represent a sort of perfectionism.
