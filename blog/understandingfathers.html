i only had enough time to focus on one thing. and the one thing i chose to focus on was bars. and the only way to write bars is to keep learning about yourself. and the only way to learn about yourself is to live. and the only way to live is in the moment. the fundamental truth that feels like superficial truth because when heard without living it it is not understood and the only way to understand it one day is to keep living meaning the moment in front of us is blank the moment before we enter it. we haven't figured out how to animate without frames. that has always been confusing to me emotionally, and as i learned more about myself, i realized that emotion was associated with the intellectual interpretation. so there is some combined intelligence of matching the correct emotions with the correct thought. sorting it out but very quickly because the moment in front of us is blank. what is our frame rate? what is the rate at which life moments carousel past us? slide show. with physical slides. or whatever the abstraction of physical is. probably.

the next moment has landed after i have found a spot to land. finding a spot to land that is safe takes time to learn, especially when you face something that contrasts your reality.

this is my relationship with hip hop. i already had the flow, i developed it to a good enough place, so that i could sit back, relax and write bars one day. again, read that back. it may not be now that i can write bars relaxedly, but that's the point. the goal is to get there and the means is to live life without guessing too much what the next frame will be. when you have lots of flow with few bars, you know how to speak but don't have anything to say. in order to have something to say, it should be as close to the truth that you can make it. once you know it's truth, you know. the challenge is to trust that. or create evidence to help you trust that. which brings me back to the next frame. we are in this freestyle together, learning how to land the next bar, the next word, the next syllable, or part of syllable, 16ths, 32nds, 64ths, always dividing by two, before or after, attacking or relaxing which actually push you the other way harder, it's about tightening that balance not by practicing how to balance, but learning what your center is. learning how to balance is flow. having a center to balance around is bars. this is the duality of hip hop. people know what bars sound like so they try to recreate them with flow-type elements: word choice, cadence, word order, placement of references (both cultural and those determined by the bar structure related to word order). again it's like learning programming without making a program. you can do it, but then your not learning how to program, you are learning a language and learning a language without having something to say is what we have to fight. the reason that gxfr album has to be heard from start to finish to enjoy it fully. west in the beginning, with a perfectly casual foreplay with the beat, leaving the listener wondering if this is the verse or if it's still the hype. that's when you know that the hype is real, when you can't tell which one is which.

 

in so many words, gxfr is real hip hop in 2020 and most likely for awhile, even though it helped a tiny bit that trump lost the election.
