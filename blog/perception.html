self-awareness is self observing self. mindfulness is self-awareness without judgment, or objective self-awareness.

self <---- observing ----- self

i often get caught in the gap of observing myself, and my process of healing is integrating those two (the observer and the observed).

internalization results in self-awareness with judgment, or subjective self-awareness. the subjectivity of the observer and the subjectivity of the self may be misaligned. this causes pain, discomfort, or trauma.

integration results in objective self-awareness. the subjectivity of the observer and the subjectivity of the self are aligned. this brings me clarity, joy and inspiration.

integration can be thought of as a connection between the observer and the observed, but with the subjectivity of the self instead of internalization of others'.

in defense of internalization, it does provide a lot of contrast to learn and grow from. the precision of my perception is learned through a variety of perspectives. the truth of self is a heavy intellectual material and tends to draw me in. i keep moving forward trusting that the truth will pull me into orbit, peacefully, so i can easily observe it. internalization is a process toward integration. think of internalization as a poor rough first approximation, the first layer of our self-understanding, itself being completely useless but as we integrate, that initial perception passes through more and more layers of self-understanding, refining and correcting itself along the way until it hits orbit.
